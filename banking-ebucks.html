FNB eBucks.

South Africa's most rewarding bank programme - if you know how to work it. Here is exactly what each level unlocks and how to climb fast.

How reward levels work

eBucks is the rewards programme from First National Bank (FNB) and RMB Private Bank, launched in 2000 as South Africa's first bank loyalty programme. The value anchor is 10 eBucks = R1.00. eBucks are earned when you spend on your qualifying FNB card, and the rate you earn depends on your account type and your reward level.

For FNB Easy and Aspire account holders, you qualify for eBucks by meeting basic account requirements and start earning when you spend on your FNB card. For FNB Premier, Private Clients, Private Wealth, and RMB Private Bank clients, the programme uses a points-and-level system. You collect points by doing your everyday banking, and those points move you up through reward levels (1 to 5). The higher your level, the more eBucks you earn per rand spent.

Key qualifying requirements for all accounts: your account must be active and in good standing, and you must log into the FNB or RMB Private Bank app at least once per month using your own banking profile. Higher account tiers also require a minimum monthly deposit into your transactional account.

The virtual card fuel strategy

Paying with your FNB virtual card - rather than your physical card - at partner forecourts is one of the most valuable tactics in the eBucks system. FNB specifically rewards virtual card usage at Engen forecourts, with fuel rewards that are higher than physical card rates at the same level.

At higher reward levels, the per-litre fuel reward from Engen via virtual card is significantly more than standard card rewards - always verify the current rate in your FNB app as figures update annually. The habit to build from day one: when you fill up at Engen, use your phone or virtual card number, not the physical card.

For FNB Premier, Private Clients, Private Wealth, and RMB Private Bank clients, using the virtual card at Checkers, Clicks, and Engen specifically earns maximum rewards and can also qualify you for weekly Starbucks coffee vouchers. This is a structural feature of the programme, not a campaign.

Streaming and subscription cashbacks

Route all your streaming and subscription payments through your FNB virtual card. At higher reward levels, you can earn up to 40% back in eBucks on selected eBucks partners. The eBucks platform also confirms up to 15% back at Pick n Pay, SPAR, and Clicks for qualifying clients.

This is a structural benefit, not a one-off promotion. If your streaming subscriptions are charged to a different card or payment method, you are leaving recurring monthly value unclaimed. Moving these over typically takes less than 10 minutes across all providers.

The eBucks Shop offers up to 40% off on selected deals, and eBucks Travel gives you up to 40% off on selected travel bookings including domestic and international flights and car hire.

App engagement - the gating rule you cannot miss

FNB has a hard requirement that often catches people off guard: you must log into the FNB or RMB Private Bank app at least once per month using your own banking profile. This is one of the qualifying conditions for the programme. Skipping this in any given month can affect your qualification for that month's rewards.

For Premier, Private Clients, Private Wealth, and RMB Private Bank clients, points are also collected through everyday banking actions: using the app, holding FNB Connect SIM products, having FNB Life insurance, and managing your account well. The more consistently you engage, the higher your reward level and the more you earn per rand spent.

Use the Track My Rewards section in the app to see exactly which behaviours you have completed, which are still open, and what moving up a level would add to your monthly earnings. Treat it as a monthly checklist, not just a balance screen.

Groceries and everyday partner spend

Beyond fuel and streaming, eBucks has a wide partner network for everyday spend. Pick n Pay and SPAR are listed as qualifying grocery partners, with confirmed rewards for eligible purchases. These earn rates stack on top of any card rewards, meaning a single Pick n Pay transaction can earn across multiple reward categories simultaneously.

The eBucks partner network also covers electronics, lifestyle purchases, and travel. Check the eBucks platform (ebucks.com) or the FNB app for the full current partner list, as new partners and campaigns are added throughout the year. Earnings from all categories accumulate in your single eBucks balance and can be redeemed across eBucks Shop, eBucks Travel, partner stores, and electronic channels.

Travel perks and private banking access

eBucks Travel provides discounts of up to 40% off on selected deals across domestic and international flights, car hire, and accommodation. Air travel discounts can be booked through the eBucks Travel engine and redeemed against your eBucks balance.

FNB has confirmed air travel partnerships that have historically included Kulula and Emirates, though the active partner list updates. Always book through the eBucks Travel engine rather than directly with the airline to capture the eBucks discount on qualifying bookings.

Verify and track

eBucks reward rates, partner lists, and qualifying requirements are updated periodically. Always confirm current earn rates in the FNB app or on ebucks.com before planning spending around a specific reward. The programme has over 25 years of operation and a broad partner network - but the specific rates in effect today are what matter for your decisions.
