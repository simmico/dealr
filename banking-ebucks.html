FNB eBucks.

South Africa's most rewarding bank programme — if you know how to work it. Here is exactly what each level unlocks and how to climb fast.

How reward levels work

FNB tracks your engagement through a behaviour grid that resets every calendar month. What you do this month determines your reward level — from 1 to 5 — for the following month. The higher your level at the start of the month, the larger the cashback percentages across every spend category.

The value anchor is straightforward: 10 eBucks = R1.00. At Level 5, that translates to meaningful real-rand savings across fuel, streaming, groceries, travel, and everyday spending — stacked across a full month.

Level behaviours include things like logging into the FNB app, paying with your virtual card, managing your account debt-to-limit ratio, and completing specific milestone goals on the Track My Rewards interface. The more consistently you engage, the faster your level rises.

The virtual card fuel strategy

This is the most valuable single tactic in the eBucks system. FNB's virtual card — available via the banking app — earns fuel cashback at a different rate than your physical card when used at Engen forecourts.

At Level 5, you can earn up to R8.00 back per litre at Engen using your virtual FNB card. On a 50-litre fill-up, that is R400 back in eBucks — effectively cutting your fuel cost nearly in half on a top-tier account.

The rule is simple: when you fill up at Engen, tap your phone or use your virtual card number rather than the physical card. If you are not yet at Level 5, the strategy still applies — the per-litre rate scales with your level, so the habit is worth building from Day 1.

Streaming and subscription cashbacks

Ensure that all your streaming services — Netflix, Spotify, YouTube Premium, and similar subscriptions — are billed directly to your FNB virtual card. At Level 5, you earn up to 40% back in eBucks on these payments.

This is not a promotion or a one-off campaign. It is a structural feature of the eBucks programme. If your streaming subscriptions run through a different card or PayPal, you are leaving a significant amount on the table every single month.

Moving these over takes about 10 minutes and the returns stack month after month. On a household that pays for Netflix, Spotify, and one or two other subscriptions, the annual eBucks value can easily exceed R1,500 at Level 5.

App engagement — the gating rule you cannot miss

FNB has a hard requirement that often catches people off guard: you must log into the FNB app at least once per month and actively engage with the Track My Rewards section. Skipping this can cost you points that drop your level for the following month.

Think of the app not just as a banking tool but as the control panel for your rewards. The Track My Rewards interface shows exactly which behaviours you have completed, which are pending, and what the impact of closing each gap would be on your level. Use it the way a frequent flyer tracks miles — it is that deliberate.

Travel perks and private banking access

Level 4 and 5 eBucks holders unlock access to the eBucks Travel engine, which provides up to 40% discount on domestic flights, international travel bookings, and car rentals. These discounts apply on top of standard promotions and are redeemable against your eBucks balance.

Clients on FNB Private Clients and Private Wealth accounts unlock additional cross-border structures and offshore banking capabilities through the Global Account Concierge — a service designed for those with international assets, foreign income, or global travel patterns.

Verify and track

All eBucks information, level details, partner promotions, and redemption options are maintained on FNB's official platforms. Reward rates are updated annually, so always confirm current percentages before planning a major spend.
