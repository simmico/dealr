Standard Bank UCount.

Tier your rewards higher, fill up at Caltex, and redirect what you earn straight into a tax-free investment - UCount has genuine wealth-building potential at Tier 4.

How UCount tiers work

Standard Bank UCount Rewards operates on a five-tier system (Tiers 1 to 5). Your tier is determined by tiering points you accumulate each month through your Standard Bank product holdings, spending behaviour, and banking activity. The more Standard Bank products you hold across different categories - transactional account, credit card, home loan, vehicle finance, insurance, investments - and the more actively you use them, the more tiering points you earn and the higher your tier climbs.

The value anchor is: rewards points are earned as a percentage of what you spend. The key insight is that the same rand spend at Tier 5 earns up to 20 times more than at Tier 1 in the credit card rewards category. Moving your tier upward is the highest-leverage action in the UCount system.

There are six categories of rewards: Card Rewards (general spend), Choose Your Own Rewards (CYOR for groceries, fashion, or lifestyle), Fuel Rewards, Retailer Rewards, Standard Bank Mobile Data Rewards, and Goals and Gains (in-app platform).

Card rewards earn rates by tier

For general card spend at any store or rewards retailer, the earn rate scales from 0.25% (Tier 1 credit card) up to 1.00% (Tier 5 credit card) of your qualifying spend, up to a spend cap of R50,000 per cycle. Debit card earn rates are lower at each tier. Routing your everyday spending through your Standard Bank credit card - rather than debit - earns a significantly higher rate at every tier level.

Fuel rewards - earn per litre at the pump

UCount has a dedicated fuel rewards category for purchases at participating fuel retailers. The earn rate scales from 30 cents per litre (Tier 1 credit card) up to R5 per litre (Tier 5 credit card). Debit card rates are lower at each tier, ranging from 10 cents to R1 per litre.

You can earn double the fuel rewards for your tiering level if you meet five criteria simultaneously: all your spending across the fixed cycle goes through your Standard Bank credit card, all your fuel spend is at a participating fuel retailer, you hold an active Standard Bank transactional current account in good standing, and you hold at least two other Standard Bank products in different categories. This double-reward structure makes consistent use of partner fuel stations meaningfully more valuable.

The strategic move: identify your nearest participating fuel retailer, make it your consistent fill-up point, and use your Standard Bank credit card (not debit) for every fill. This single habit, sustained over a year, compounds into significant rewards - especially if you qualify for the double-reward criteria.

Choose Your Own Rewards - CYOR grocery stacking

CYOR is UCount's high-earn category. You choose one rewards focus: groceries, fashion, or lifestyle. Your chosen category earns at a substantially higher rate than card rewards. For groceries, the earn rate scales from 2% (Tier 1 credit card, other CYOR retailers) up to 20% (Tier 5 credit card, other CYOR retailers).

For spending specifically at Checkers, Checkers Hyper, Shoprite, and Shoprite Hyper in-store, the rates are boosted: up to 30% back in rewards points at Tier 5 with a credit card. For online purchases at Checkers Sixty60 and Shoprite Sixty60, the rate goes even higher - up to 40% back in rewards points at Tier 5 with a credit card.

The CYOR spend cap is the lower of 20% of your total card spend, or R3,000 on your chosen category per cycle. If groceries is your chosen category, concentrate your grocery spend at Checkers or Shoprite - specifically Sixty60 online ordering for maximum rewards - and pay with your Standard Bank credit card every time.

Directing rewards into investments

This is UCount's most distinctive feature - and the one that separates it from every other major SA bank rewards programme. You can direct your accrued UCount reward balance into a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) or other Standard Bank investment structures rather than spending them on vouchers or cashbacks.

Rather than spending points on day-to-day purchases, routing them into a TFSA compounds them inside a tax-sheltered investment vehicle. For anyone with a long investment horizon, this difference in trajectory is substantial. If you are already maximising your annual TFSA contribution limit, surplus points can route into other investment pockets - still compounding, still growing.

Signature and Infinite concierge access

Standard Bank Signature and Infinite cardholders unlock access to a dedicated lifestyle and travel concierge service alongside the VISA Signature and Infinite privileges that come with those card tiers. The concierge handles restaurant reservations, event ticketing, travel itinerary support, and personal requests. Both the UCount programme and the VISA-level card benefits operate in parallel.
